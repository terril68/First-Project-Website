Animals provide people social support during the pandemic

A story by Teresa Liu

The COVID-19 pandemic forced Americans to stay home, so many have turned to a furry friend as a way to cope with stress and loneliness. This resulted in a nationwide surge of pet adoptions. However, as data from Shelter Animals Count shows, the annual number of animals entering shelters has been steadily declining since the start of the pandemic, because the population of stray animals and other sources of admission have also experienced decreases.

As Chart 1 demonstrates, demand for adoptions peaked in 2020, when the COVID-19 first reached the US. The number of adoptions actually fell in 2020 and 2021, compared with the previous years. This was due to a decrease in the total number of animals entering shelters and rescue groups. In other words, the number of people willing to adopt outpaced the number of animals available for adoption.
